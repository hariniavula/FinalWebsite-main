Academic References

  • The Long-Term and Spillover Effects of Price Promotions on Retailing Platforms: Evidence from a Large Randomized Experiment on Alibaba


    The paper explores how discounts and promotional offers influence consumer behavior on major e-commerce platforms. Through a large-scale field experiment on Alibaba, the authors found that while promotions boost short-term sales, they also shift purchasing habits over time as consumers become more sensitive to future discounts. This supports our project’s analysis (Viz 3) of how incentives like discounts and promo codes affect purchase amounts, providing a strong foundation for understanding how promotional strategies shape spending and customer loyalty.


    Dai, Hengchen, Ginger Zhe Jin, and Andrew K. T. Nguyen. The Long-Term and Spillover Effects of Price Promotions on Retailing Platforms: Evidence from a Large Randomized Experiment on Alibaba. SSRN Electronic Journal, 2017. https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3029707
    .

  • Demand Dynamics in the Seasonal Goods Industry: An Empirical Analysis


    The paper examines how consumers make purchasing decisions across different seasons as prices and discounts change. The authors show that shoppers weigh paying higher prices early against waiting for discounts later in the season, revealing how timing and availability influence behavior. Their findings suggest that seasonality and pricing strategies** affect demand, supporting our analysis (Viz 2) of seasonal shopping trends. This study strengthens our project by providing a research-based understanding of how timing and sales incentives shape consumer patterns.

    Nair, Harikesh S. Demand Dynamics in the Seasonal Goods Industry: An Empirical Analysis. Marketing Science, vol. 26, no. 3, 2007, pp. 314–333. ResearchGate, https://www.researchgate.net/publication/256036420.
